# Air-Quality-Analysis-Using-Python-

SUMMARY
This project analyzes global air quality using 10,000 records collected from major 20 cities around
the world. The dataset contains six air pollutants (PM2.5, PM10, NO₂, SO₂, CO, and O₃) and three
weather factors (temperature, humidity, and wind speed). The Air Quality Index (AQI) serves as the primary metric for assessing air quality levels. Health
analysis shows that most records fall in medium or high health risk categories. Several machine learning models were built. Random Forest Regression gave the best AQI
prediction (R² = 0.999), and Gradient Boosting Classification gave the best AQI category
prediction (Accuracy = 0.998). Based on the results, the report suggests PM2.5 control strategies, special actions for high-risk
cities, and use of smart monitoring technologies to improve air quality
Key Findings:
 Average AQI: 204.6 (indicating generally unhealthy air quality).  Dominant Pollutant: PM2.5 is the dominant pollutant driving AQI in 76.2% of cases.  AQI Distribution: Unhealthy (65%), Moderate (16%), Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups (14%), Good (5%).  Highest Risk Cities: Cities like Mumbai and Dubai have the highest average AQI, indicating
higher health risks.  Health Risks: High risk in 539 records, Medium in 8393, Low in 1064 – meaning most days
pose some health concerns, especially for vulnerable groups like children and those with
respiratory issues. Seasonal and weekly cycles show higher pollution in summer months and
mid-week days. Modeling Results:
 Best AQI Prediction (Regression): Random Forest with R² of 0.999 (nearly perfect
predictions).  Best AQI Category Prediction (Classification): Gradient Boosting with 99.8% accuracy. 
Recommendations:
 Focus on PM2.5: Euro 6 standards, EV promotion, industrial filters.  City-Specific: For high-AQI cities (Mumbai, Dubai): More monitors, green belts, public
transport.  Tech/Policy: AI forecasting, emission tests, early warnings.  Public Health: Alerts for vulnerable groups. This analysis highlights the urgent need for global action on air pollution to protect public health.
